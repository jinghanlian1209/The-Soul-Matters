To love without pretending.

June 1, 2026Los Angeles 7 min read

A friend of mine once wrote me a seven-page letter. We had known each other for more than eleven years, yet we had not seen each other for eight.

He transferred to another high school, then moved to the United States for college. Now he is doing his PhD at Harvard.

There was one passage in his letter that stayed with me:

“Then you came. Not a colleague, not someone impressed by what I can do, but you, who knew me when I was a child, who is fond of me and not of my mind, who crossed the airport at a run to reach me after eight years of almost nothing. You were glad to see me. I don’t think you know what that did. After years of being valued, at best, for my intelligence and otherwise left to myself by the human side of this world, to have someone be plainly glad I exist hands me back my own personhood. For a moment, I am a person again, and not a capability.”

I have thought about those words many times since.

Perhaps this is how I have always tried to love people, with something close to the simplicity of a child.

I believe there are two parts to a person. One is the original self, the quiet foundation of who they are. The other is the self they gradually build through talent, knowledge, beauty, money, achievement, and social position.

The second part may be impressive, even dazzling. But I am always more curious about the first. I want to understand the part of a person that cannot be easily replaced. Not what they own, what they have accomplished, or how the world measures them, but who they are beneath all of it.

For example, perhaps you are a musician. You can play several instruments, write beautiful music, earn a great deal of money, or move easily through rooms filled with famous people. All of that may be fascinating, but to me, it is still only a bonus.

What truly opens the door to my heart is the way you look at the world.

How do you treat people when there is nothing to gain? Why did you choose this life? What passes through your mind when you see something beautiful? What makes you tender, frightened, hopeful, or alive?

Someone else may possess the same talent. They may even be more skilled than you. No one can promise that they play the piano better than every other person in the world. Talent, however extraordinary, is never entirely unique. But the way you think is yours alone.

It has been shaped by everything you have loved, lost, feared, endured, and remembered. All of those experiences gather quietly inside you and become the person you are.

That matters to me.

So when I ask about your thoughts or your inner world, there is no practical purpose behind it. I am not trying to gain anything from you. I am curious simply because it is you.In my relationships, whether with friends or with a partner, I rarely ask people for very much. My own life is already full. I have work to do, places to go, hobbies to lose myself in, and more thoughts than I know where to put. I do not expect another person to solve my life for me.

I only want the people around me to feel free to be themselves, openly, happily, and vividly.

To keep you in my life means that I love you. And to love you means that I want you to be able to remain yourself in front of me.

Some people believe that a man should always take the lead, be fearless, and take care of the woman he loves. But I do not see love that way. I think we are equal.

My partner does not have to be strong all the time. He does not need to perform confidence for me. He is allowed to be tired, uncertain, or weak. He can rest in my arms, and I can take care of him too.

Of course, when someone wants to protect me, support me, or give something to me, I receive it with love and gratitude. But I do not want affection to feel like an obligation. I do not want to push someone into loving me in a particular way, or make them feel that they must constantly prove themselves.

I can take care of myself.

Liking you is my business, so I will love you in the way that feels honest to me. But liking me is yours. You are free to decide how you love me, and how much.

The strange and beautiful thing is that, although I rarely ask for anything, the people around me are always giving me something.

Their time. Their care. Their attention. Their letters. Their quiet acts of tenderness.

Perhaps that is proof that they, too, possess a beautiful original self.

And perhaps it is also true that the people who genuinely love you do not always need to be asked. Love moves toward you on its own.

So all I can do is express myself plainly and directly, to say what I feel while it is still alive inside me, and then leave the rest untouched.

To love without pretending.

To speak without bargaining.

To be glad that someone exists, without asking them to become anything more.

A line I want to keep

Made with too many thoughts and just enough courage.
