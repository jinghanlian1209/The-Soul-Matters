See You Soon

The people who are meant to meet again will find their way back to each other.

June 11, 2026School of Public Affair4 min read

Peter is graduating.

Today was the last time we worked together. When our shift ended, he came running over to find me. I knew he had come to say goodbye.

I still remember my first days here, half a year ago. Everything was unfamiliar, and I could barely express myself clearly. I stood there alone, completely lost, not knowing what to do.

He walked over and asked if I wanted to draw Christmas illustrations on paper bags with him.

And just like that, he became my best friend in the office.

Later, I was surprised to find out that he was actually an introvert. I asked him why, then, he had been the one to come and talk to me on my very first day. He said it was because he could tell how nervous I was.

I laughed and teased him, “An INTJ only has to take one step toward me. As an ENFP, I’ll take the other ninety-nine.”

I know he loves Boston.

Maybe he will never come back.

And me?

Will I stay here? Will I even be able to stay?

I don’t know.

He started saying all sorts of things at once. That he was so glad he had met me, that working with me had made him so happy, that he felt lucky he had taken this job. Then came, “Wish you all the best.” May your internship search go well. May your work go well. May everything in your life go well.

I laughed and cut him off because I was afraid that if he kept talking, I would start crying.

I told him, “You’re wishing for too many things. You should wish for something easier to make happen, like when we’re going to see each other next.”

We laughed and said, “See you soon.”

But both of us knew that, in this long life ahead of us, perhaps we would never see each other again.

I turned my head away.

We are all adults now, I told myself. Less drama. More maturity. More acceptance.

It’s okay. I like it here.

No matter what happens, I will find a way to stay.

Keep walking forward, girl.

The people who are meant to meet again will find their way back to each other.

A line I want to keep

Keep walking forward, girl.

Made with too many thoughts and just enough courage.
