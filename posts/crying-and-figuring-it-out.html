Give the World Time

Don’t trap yourself in that role.

June 26, 2026Westwood6 min read
☀ warm♫ All to Well♡ overwhelmed, still moving

So I’m writing this in a good mood. Living abroad alone really does come with both bitterness and sweetness. Somehow, it grows you.

The other day, I called my dad and told him I wanted to go home. He said, “Then come back. Come home and get some rest.”

I said no. I have to deal with all of this on my own. I need a result.

Then he suddenly said, “A guitar string snaps when it’s pulled too tight. Do you know that being strong and being arrogant are only one step apart?” “You always think you’re the smartest person in the world. Independent, tolerant, interesting. You always think you don’t need help. You don’t believe other people can really help you. And at the same time, you stubbornly believe you can help others, understand everything, hold everything.”

“Everyone has difficulties. Your mom and I do too. But you only believe you can overcome your own problems while helping others. You don’t believe others can do the same for you. In a way, you find joy in giving. But when others give to you, you feel guilty. Isn’t it because, deep down, you don’t believe others can be as selfless, kind, and sincere as you are? Or even more capable than you?”

“But you’re just an ordinary person. You are not that strong. You need to admit that you need help. And you also need to admit that many times, you cannot help others. You cannot fully understand another person’s situation.”

“Don’t trap yourself in that role. Don’t think of yourself as some kind of saint. Truly strong people dare to calmly admit their own limits.”

That made sense.

So I met up with many friends and told them about my confusion. I scheduled coffee chats with seniors and people working in the industries I’m interested in.

And I realized that people can be so sweet and generous. Many of them understand tech and consulting much better than I do. Many of them understand life much better than I do, too.

The consequence, of course, was that I kept working in front of my laptop until two or three in the morning. But it was worth it, paths became clearer. I slowly climbed out of my own dead end. I admitted that I’m only an ordinary person. And I admitted that this world has so many kind people who are more capable than me and still willing to help.

And the root of all this thinking was: I saw a beautiful soul. Just a soul. I was deeply moved by it. A strange feeling, impossible to explain with logic. I couldn’t help wanting to get closer. But I was afraid my thorns would hurt it. And I didn’t want to walk away either.

So I thought, maybe I need to put myself back together first. To become a more grounded and coherent version of myself, so I can love someone better.

Or,

maybe I don’t have to become whole before I can love. I just want to keep growing while I love.

To become a better person, I still have a long way to go, and so much to learn.

To admit my weaknesses. To accept that not everything gives you a result immediately just because you tried hard. To give myself time. To give others time. And to give the world a little time, too.

Mom said, don’t rush the grapes into ripening. If they are yours, they will ripen in their own time. If they are not yours, you cannot force them. Think, then take action. Then think again, then keep taking action. Do everything you can. Whatever the result is, you won’t regret it.
