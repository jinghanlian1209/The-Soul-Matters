Thirteen Hours Away

My first Lunar New Year away from home.

February 16, 20261515 Colby5 min read

One by one, photos of family reunion dinners began filling my feed.

Meanwhile, it was still the early hours of the morning here in Los Angeles. This year has been so full that choosing just nine photos for a recap felt almost impossible.

I moved from Hangzhou to LA. Home used to be a three-hour flight away. Now it is thirteen.

There are nights when I lie awake wondering if I made the right decision. What kind of view could possibly be worth missing the changing seasons of home? And then I wake up, see the California sun pouring through my window, and somehow my answer becomes clear again.

Every bit of it has been worth it.

I am so grateful I came here.

So much of what I have struggled through cannot really be understood by anyone else.

But through it all, I have discovered something unexpected.

I am far braver, stronger, and more optimistic than I ever knew. Turns out, I really am a pretty amazing kid.

For the first time, I can confidently believe that words like *cute, pretty, interesting, kind, smart, clever,* and *positive* all belong to me too.

This year helped me see myself more clearly. It helped me see the world more clearly too.

Every thought that reached a deeper part of my soul, every unexpected discovery, every quiet shift in the way I see life filled me with genuine joy.

I have been lucky enough to meet so many wonderful people here. We have been confused together. And we have grown together.

Maybe that is what this year has really been about. The happiness, the excitement, the heartbreak, the sadness.

None of it was wasted.

Each moment became another vivid stroke across the pages of my life, impossible to replace with anyone else's.

In the new year, I hope I spend less time overthinking and doubting myself. I hope I keep moving forward with courage.

If there is someone you want to see, go see them. If there is something you want to do, go do it.

And if you make mistakes along the way...

So what?

You are already brave enough.

You are already interesting enough.

Happy Lunar New Year's Eve, from Los Angeles.
