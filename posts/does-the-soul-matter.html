Love & Souls

Does the Soul Really Matter?

On ambition, tenderness, and the parts of us that refuse to become practical.

July 1, 2026Los Angeles6 min read

I thought my dream was simply to live a happy life, and the reason I pushed myself so hard was responsibility.

For mom’s illness. To give back. To make sure the people I love could live well.

But when they gently lifted that weight off my shoulders and told me, “Come home and rest. It’s okay.” I finally saw the real source of my pain. It wasn’t just responsibility. It was ambition. Or my abilities still haven’t caught up with the size of my ambition. The world is so big. I’m so young. I can’t settle.

Through countless unbearable nights alone, I shook with fear and anxiety. In countless dreams, I found myself back on my carefree college campus, missing home, missing warmth, missing the comfort of someone’s embrace, waking up to pillows soaked with tears.

But they were only memories. I can’t go back. And I don’t want to, either.

My ex was calling again and again, sometimes I questioned whether any of this was worth it. Looking at the happiness I could have by simply taking one step back, I found myself asking a painfully sentimental question: Does the soul really matter? I wanted nothing more than to hide in a warm embrace and cry.

But if I had never seen the sun, perhaps I could have endured the darkness.

When I say I would do anything for Mr. Right, I don’t mean I would abandon myself for love. I mean I’m simply not someone who gives up on the people I love. And I believe I can accomplish whatever I truly decide to pursue, not sacrifice everything for someone else.

The hardest problems are the moments when someone cannot understand my soul, my sense of responsibility, my ambition, or the bigger world I see. I don’t need someone to shield me from the rain. I need someone willing to walk into the storm with me.

I’m too picky, too demanding to settle for an ordinary embrace. I can’t be satisfied with companionship that merely numbs the pain or offers temporary emotional comfort.

What I search for is a soul. And somehow, in the endless crowd, I recognized a beautiful soul at first glance. Sincere. Pure. Strong.

Its beauty had nothing to do with appearance, talent, or achievement. Just the soul alone was enough to leave me completely in awe. I loved that soul the way I love my own. I understood that soul the way I understand myself. It made no logical sense, yet it felt undeniably real.

It became a mirror. Through that, I saw the person I wanted to become. And I finally knew that what I had been searching for all along truly existed.

But I can’t hold him back. Just as I can’t hold myself back.

A soul like that… I ache for him.

But I also know that if I pity too much, it would mean I don’t truly believe in his strength. Real respect for someone strong isn’t trying to shield them from every storm. It’s believing they can walk through it on their own. And asking him to become the rope that pulls me out of my own swamp would be too heavy a burden. Too unfair to him.

A soul like that… I ache for myself too.

But I also know that if I lose myself in the comfort of a temporary embrace, if I fail to grow into who I’m meant to become because of it, that would be unfair to myself.

A soul this precious deserves the very best. Just as I do.

This is my lesson to learn. So even while trembling with fear, I chose to cut off every path back. I have to face this myself. I have to grow flesh and blood onto my own soul.

Don’t be afraid, young girl. The sun has risen. And I will save myself from the fire, again and again.

A line I want to keep

“I do not want a smaller life merely because it would be easier to explain.”

Made with too many thoughts and just enough courage.
