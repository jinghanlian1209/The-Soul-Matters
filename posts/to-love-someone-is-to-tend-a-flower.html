Love & Souls

Does the Soul Really Matter?

On ambition, tenderness, and the parts of us that refuse to become practical.

June 10, 2026Los Angeles10 min read

There is a line I have always loved: To love someone is to tend a flower.

I don’t know whether I should consider myself lucky or unlucky.

When I think about it, even though I am already twenty-three, I have never truly stepped outside of school. I grew up on university campuses. Maybe that is why I am so good at keeping people in my life.

My friendships last five years, ten years, sometimes fifteen.

Even my exes are good people. My life has never had those dramatic, messy love stories. Most of my relationships ended simply because we began walking at different speeds, or toward different places. Even after we broke up, they would still tell me, “I’m not going to disappear. Call me anytime if you need me.”

When I think about it that way, I have been very lucky. I have never truly been hurt in a relationship.

So I trust people. And I know how to love them. But perhaps that is also my misfortune.

The world is so big, and I cannot stay inside a university forever. I still don’t know how to survive in this fast, disposable adult world.

I am curious about the world, about different people, different cultures, and different ways of thinking. I am open to new experiences. I am willing to meet new people.

Meeting someone new can be exciting. It is a little like buying a new book.

People told me that dating apps were the best place to meet interesting strangers. I said, all right, I’ll try.

But then I looked at those profiles, at people displayed like products on a shelf. I know it is efficient. Maybe the problem is simply me.

I hate relationships built around usefulness. I hate meeting someone with a hidden purpose. I hate filtering people. I hate treating a human being as a product, a tool, or a list of qualities.

Adults are already busy with their own lives, their own friends, their own responsibilities. Making room for a stranger takes so much time and energy. Getting to know someone has become expensive, while replacing them has become almost effortless.

One swipe, and there is someone else.

There are endless people in the world. You can even ghost someone without feeling particularly guilty.

But finding someone to love is not the same as looking for a job. You cannot simply say, “This one isn’t moving forward,” and move on to the next candidate.

I have always wanted to exchange sincerity for sincerity. Of course, I know the world is complicated. People are complicated. And I happen to be especially sensitive.

I can see the calculation in their eyes.

I know they like that I am pretty, interesting, and understanding. I know that sometimes they like me the way people like a beautiful book cover, without caring very much about what is actually written inside. I can see the strategies, the games between men and women, the quiet evaluations and comparisons.

Once, I met a guy for coffee. He kept asking me questions. He asked about my views on love, my family, even how my parents met. At first, I was delighted. I love talking about these things. I love talking about how I see the world, telling my parents’ love story, sharing all the strange little thoughts that live inside my head.

But later, I looked into his eyes. There was not a trace of feeling in them. Only calculation. That was when I realized he was not truly curious about me. He was sitting there like an interviewer holding a score sheet, grading every answer I gave.

In that moment, I thought: How boring.

The world keeps moving faster, and I keep getting more tired. Every new connection takes an enormous amount of energy from me.

I cannot look at a book and stop at the cover. I want to read it slowly. I want to understand a person. I want to observe their soul. It is like finding one tree in a forest, then beginning to water it. Watching it. Waiting beside it. Slowly moving closer. And once I have truly started watering one tree, it is difficult for me to walk away, or to water ten other trees at the same time.

I don’t like it when people ask me what my type is. I don’t have a type. Every person is unique. A “type” is just another kind of filter, and there will always be thousands of people who meet the same criteria.

Love, for me, has to be felt. I have to feel the soul of the person in front of me. I love you not because you meet a certain standard, but because something about who you are has touched me deeply.

My friends always ask me, “You’re pretty, you’re kind, and so many people like you. Why are you still single?”

So I stopped and asked myself: What am I actually looking for in a relationship?

I don’t think I need cheap companionship anymore, or empty reassurance. I have heard “You are so pretty” countless times. But those words only mean something when they come from someone I love.

I don’t need someone to talk to simply to pass the time. I don’t need a playmate. My life is already full. I have friends to talk to and people to go out with. When I am alone, I can write poems, read books, or paint. Money and recognition are things I can work for myself, if I decide I want them.

I don’t want someone I truly love to become a tool in my life. Because of love, he may choose to give. But also because of love, I don’t want to keep taking from him.

Of course I want companionship. Of course I want someone to have dinner with, someone to watch movies with, someone to see the world with, sharing three meals a day through all four seasons. Of course I want a pair of arms around me.

But not because I am lonely. Not because just anyone will do.

I want to need you because I love you, not love you because I need someone. I want a beautiful soul. I want to see you, and I want you to see me too. I want that embrace to come from someone who has seen the softness beneath my strength and still chooses to hold me. When problems come, I want us to solve the problem, not remove the person.

Not swipe left and look for the next one.

Eventually, I deleted the apps. I was so tired.

People who appeared only briefly in my life consumed too much of my energy. I know now that I will never adapt to that kind of world. And I don’t want to.

Mystery has never come from strategies, games, or carefully calculated distance. Mystery is simply you being yourself. It is the depth of your soul. The way you have your own thoughts about the world. The way you make me endlessly curious, wanting to keep discovering more. It is the feeling that makes me want to keep you in my life. The feeling that pulls me closer, until I can no longer stop myself.

I only want to know someone slowly.

I want to know their soul.

I want to choose one tree and water it patiently.

To love someone is to tend a flower.

God, in a time like this, can a hopeless idealist like me still be happy? I wonder about it often.

What if I never find that person?

Well. Finding someone to love is not the same as finding a job. I am not in a hurry. I will look slowly.

And even if I never find you, I can still eat alone, watch movies alone, and travel through the world alone, sharing my life with all its ordinary meals and changing seasons.

But I still hope I can do all of it with you.

Please come soon. I don’t want to keep doubting anymore.

A line I want to keep

A mortal life lasts only a hundred years. Love is a moment outside the order of things.

Made with too many thoughts and just enough courage.
