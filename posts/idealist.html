Little Thoughts

Idealist

journeys end in lovers meeting.

June 13, 2026Sawtelle8 min read

Maybe it’s because of the affogato. Another sleepless night. I like writing when I can’t sleep, trying to figure out what exactly is keeping me trapped in my own head. I opened the Notes and realized there were already five entries this month alone.

Ridiculous.

I always thought of myself as smart and optimistic, strong and independent. But everything has felt stuck for a long time. I don’t know what needs to be fixed more urgently: my reality, or my emotions.

Reality has always been difficult. So lately I’ve been wondering if the real problem isn’t reality itself, but the way I make sense of it.

Summer is coming. I’ve been saying goodbye too much lately. It has amplified every uncertainty I was already carrying.

They said I should become a therapist. They said my sincerity and empathy are gifts. But I am constantly torn between protecting that part of myself and giving it up.

There are too many things to solve as an adult. It feels endless. And I still don’t know how to live lightly, care lightly, connect lightly. I still struggle with relationships that exist in the gray area, even when they’re just friendships. I cherish connections more than I probably should.

But the truth is, people can only do so much.

I am afraid of crossing the boundaries of adulthood. Afraid of being too intense, too serious, too warm, too childish, too impossibly idealistic. Afraid of being too much for people. So I keep telling myself to relax, to be an adult. People come and go. They come and go.

But most of the time, I still can’t help caring. And then I start questioning myself again. Did I cross a line? Did I care too loudly? Did I forget that there is only so much I can do?

Pretending not to care is exhausting. Caring while being powerless is exhausting too. I don’t know. To love people and the world sincerely, intensely, and without calculation is sometimes so painful.

The only question I can ask myself is: will I waver? Yes, I will.

Just like every time I swear after another sleepless night that I will never drink matcha again. A thousand times, I still cannot change the fact that I love deeply.

I’ve always felt that extreme rationality can sometimes be a form of fear. People convince themselves they have already seen every possible outcome, and then call surrender wisdom. I know I cannot keep everyone. I know I cannot make everything happen. Still, for the people I truly want to keep, and the things I truly want to make real, I will do everything I can. I will keep trying even if it hurts. Even if it is hard. Even if I doubt myself.

Maybe we can only change ten percent of what happens. Maybe we can only predict ten percent of life. But that also means that when everything feels hopeless, there is still ninety percent of possibility waiting beyond what we can see, as long as we are stubborn enough to stay.

I read Kant again. To acknowledge the limits of reason is the greatest form of reason.

Maybe that’s why I still believe that journeys end in lovers meeting.

A thousand times, ten thousand times, I’m still the same hopeless, foolish idealist.

But being Shakespeare is not my dream. I don’t need more essays. I don’t need more overthinking.

What I need rn is a little progress. Even a really, really tiny one, in anything.

Or maybe just one reassuring night of sleep, which hasn’t visited me in a very long time.
